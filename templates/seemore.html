Uttar Pradesh

Taj Mahal

Agra, Uttar Pradesh

Description

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the river Yamuna in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India. It was commissioned in 1631 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favourite wife, Mumtaz Mahal; it also houses the tomb of Shah Jahan himself.

History

The Taj Mahal was built by Emperor Shah Jahan between 1632 and 1653 as a mausoleum for his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, who died during childbirth. More than 20,000 workers, sculptors, calligraphers, and artisans worked to complete this masterpiece of Mughal architecture.

Agra Fort

Agra, Uttar Pradesh

Description

Agra Fort is a historical fort in the city of Agra, a UNESCO World Heritage site located about 2.5 km northwest of the Taj Mahal. It is a massive red-sandstone structure enclosing palatial buildings, audience halls, and two mosques.

History

The fort was built primarily as a military structure by Emperor Akbar in 1565, but was later transformed into a palace by his grandson Shah Jahan. It served as the main residence of the emperors of the Mughal Dynasty until 1638 when the capital was shifted from Agra to Delhi.

Fatehpur Sikri

Agra, Uttar Pradesh

Description

Fatehpur Sikri is a city founded in 1569 by the Mughal Emperor Akbar, and served as the capital of the Mughal Empire from 1571 to 1585. It combines Hindu and Islamic architecture elements, showcasing the syncretic nature of the Mughal period.

History

Akbar built this city to honor the Sufi saint Salim Chishti, who had predicted the birth of his son. The complex includes royal palaces, courts, a mosque, private quarters, and other utility buildings. The city was abandoned due to water shortages shortly after its completion.

Itimad-ud-Daulah's Tomb

Agra, Uttar Pradesh

Description

Often referred to as the 'Baby Taj', this magnificent tomb is considered to be a draft of the Taj Mahal. It is the first structure to be entirely made out of marble in Mughal India.

History

Built between 1622 and 1628, this tomb was commissioned by Nur Jahan, the wife of Emperor Jahangir, for her father Mirza Ghiyas Beg, who was given the title Itimad-ud-Daulah (Pillar of the State). It represents a significant transition in Mughal architecture from sandstone to white marble.

Akbar's Tomb

Sikandra, Agra, Uttar Pradesh

Description

The tomb of the great Mughal Emperor Akbar, located in Sikandra, a suburb of Agra. This majestic sandstone and marble tomb reflects the emperor's architectural innovation and religious tolerance.

History

Akbar himself started the construction of this tomb in around 1600, and it was completed by his son Jahangir in 1605-1613. The tomb blends Hindu, Christian, Islamic, Buddhist, and Jain designs, reflecting Akbar's inclusive religious policy.

Jhansi Fort

Jhansi, Uttar Pradesh

Description

Jhansi Fort is a fortress located on a rocky hill called Bangira in Jhansi, Uttar Pradesh. The fort is associated with Rani Lakshmibai of Jhansi who led an armed rebellion against the British during the Indian Rebellion of 1857.

History

Built by Raja Bir Singh Deo of Orchha in 1613, the fort became a symbol of resistance during the First War of Indian Independence. Rani Lakshmibai used this fort as her stronghold and fought bravely from here before escaping the British forces.

Maharashtra

Ajanta and Ellora Caves

Aurangabad, Maharashtra

Description

These magnificent cave complexes contain some of the finest specimens of ancient Indian art and architecture. Ajanta's 29 caves feature paintings and sculptures depicting Buddhist tales, while Ellora's 34 caves include Hindu, Buddhist and Jain temples carved out of the mountainside.

History

The Ajanta caves were created between the 2nd century BCE and about 480 CE, and rediscovered in 1819. The Ellora caves were built between the 6th and 10th centuries CE. Together they represent some of the most remarkable examples of ancient Indian artistic achievement.

Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus

Mumbai, Maharashtra

Description

Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus, formerly Victoria Terminus, is a historic railway station and UNESCO World Heritage Site in Mumbai. It's an outstanding example of Victorian Gothic Revival architecture blended with Indian traditional elements.

History

Designed by British architect F.W. Stevens, it was built over 10 years starting in 1878 to commemorate Queen Victoria's Golden Jubilee. The station was renamed in 1996 after the founder of the Maratha Empire, Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj.

Gateway of India

Mumbai, Maharashtra

Description

The Gateway of India is an arch monument built during the 20th century in Mumbai. The monument was erected to commemorate the landing of King George V and Queen Mary at Apollo Bunder on their visit to India in 1911.

History

Built in Indo-Saracenic style, the foundation stone was laid in 1913, but the construction was only completed in 1924. Ironically, it later became a symbol of the end of British rule when the last British troops left India through this gateway in 1948.

Delhi

Red Fort

Delhi

Description

The Red Fort is a historic fort complex located in Delhi that served as the main residence of the Mughal Emperors. Built in 1639 by Emperor Shah Jahan, the massive red sandstone structure represents the pinnacle of Mughal architecture.

History

The fort was the residence of the Mughal emperor for nearly 200 years, until 1857. Every year on Independence Day (August 15), the Prime Minister of India hoists the national flag at the main gate of the fort and delivers a nationally broadcast speech from its ramparts.

Qutub Minar

Delhi

Description

Qutub Minar is a 73-meter tall minaret built in the early 13th century. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the tallest brick minarets in the world. The tower is an example of Indo-Islamic architecture with intricate carvings.

History

Construction of the Qutub Minar was started by Qutb-ud-din Aibak, the founder of the Delhi Sultanate, in 1199 AD and was completed by his successor Iltutmish. Made of red sandstone and marble, the tower has five distinct stories, each marked by a projecting balcony.

Humayun's Tomb

Delhi

Description

Humayun's Tomb is the tomb of the Mughal Emperor Humayun. The tomb was commissioned by Humayun's first wife and chief consort, Empress Bega Begum in 1569-70, and designed by Persian architect Mirak Mirza Ghiyas.

History

This grand mausoleum was the first garden-tomb on the Indian subcontinent, and inspired architectural innovations that culminated in the construction of the Taj Mahal. The complex encompasses the main tomb of Emperor Humayun, and numerous other subsequent burials.

India Gate

Delhi

Description

India Gate is a 42-meter-high war memorial located on the Rajpath in New Delhi. It was designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens and stands as a symbol of India's sacrifice during World War I and the Third Anglo-Afghan War.

History

Formerly known as the All India War Memorial, it was built to honor the 70,000 Indian soldiers who lost their lives fighting for the British Army during World War I. The names of 13,300 servicemen are inscribed on the gate. In 1971, following the Bangladesh Liberation War, a structure called Amar Jawan Jyoti was added underneath.

Lotus Temple

Delhi

Description

The Lotus Temple is a Bahá'í House of Worship notable for its flower-like shape. Completed in 1986, it has become one of Delhi's most visited buildings, attracting over 10,000 visitors daily due to its unique architecture.

History

Designed by Iranian architect Fariborz Sahba, the building consists of 27 free-standing marble-clad "petals" arranged in clusters of three to form nine sides. The Lotus Temple has won numerous architectural awards and has been featured in hundreds of newspaper and magazine articles.

Jama Masjid

Delhi

Description

Jama Masjid is one of the largest mosques in India with a courtyard capable of holding 25,000 devotees. The mosque was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan between 1644 and 1656 at a cost of 1 million rupees.

History

The mosque's architectural plan is similar to that of Jama Masjid, Fatehpur Sikri. The mosque was completed in 1656 AD with three great gates, four towers and two 40 m high minarets constructed of strips of red sandstone and white marble.

Rajasthan

Hawa Mahal

Jaipur, Rajasthan

Description

Hawa Mahal ("Palace of Winds") is a five-story palace built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh. The unique honeycomb facade with 953 small windows (jharokhas) was designed to allow royal ladies to observe street festivals while remaining unseen.

History

Built as an extension of the Royal City Palace, this distinctive structure was designed by Lal Chand Ustad in the form of Krishna's crown. The palace was constructed of red and pink sandstone, and features intricate latticework and detailed carvings.

Book a Visit

City Palace Jaipur

Jaipur, Rajasthan

Description

The City Palace forms one of the most famous tourist attractions and a major landmark in Jaipur. The palace complex includes Chandra Mahal and Mubarak Mahal, and other buildings. It was the seat of the Maharaja of Jaipur, the head of the Kachwaha Rajput clan.

History

Built between 1729 and 1732 by Sawai Jai Singh II, the palace complex blends Rajput, Mughal and European architectural styles. The palace was one of the first buildings completed in Jaipur after it became the capital of the Kachwaha Rajputs.

Amber Fort

Jaipur, Rajasthan

Description

Amber Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage site located on a hill overlooking Maota Lake. The fort is known for its artistic Hindu style elements with large ramparts, series of gates and cobbled paths, and overlooks Maota Lake.

History

Built by Raja Man Singh I in 1592 and expanded by successive rulers, the fort was the residence of the Rajput Maharajas and their families. The fort's magnificent blend of Hindu and Mughal architecture is a testament to the rich cultural heritage of Rajasthan.

Chittorgarh Fort

Chittorgarh, Rajasthan

Description

Chittorgarh Fort is the largest fort in India and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The fort stands on a 180-meter high hill and covers an area of 700 acres, featuring several magnificent palaces, gates, temples and two prominent commemoration towers.

History

Built by the Mauryans in the 7th century, the fort has witnessed three major sieges and stories of unparalleled bravery. It is associated with the heroic saga of Queen Padmini and her act of jauhar (self-immolation) to protect her honor from Alauddin Khilji.

Jaisalmer Fort

Jaisalmer, Rajasthan

Description

Jaisalmer Fort, also known as Sonar Quila (Golden Fort), is one of the largest fully preserved fortified cities in the world. The massive yellow sandstone walls rise from the desert and blend with the golden hues of the sand, giving it a magical appearance, especially at sunset.

History

Built in 1156 AD by the Rajput ruler Rawal Jaisal, the fort housed the palace of the royal family and also many merchant havelis. It is the second oldest fort in Rajasthan and has been the scene of many battles and sieges over its 800-year history.

Mehrangarh Fort

Jodhpur, Rajasthan

Description

Mehrangarh Fort stands 400 feet above the city of Jodhpur on a perpendicular cliff. One of the largest forts in India, it encloses several palaces known for their intricate carvings and expansive courtyards, as well as a museum that houses an impressive collection of artifacts.

History

Founded around 1459 by Rao Jodha, the fort has never been captured by enemies. The imprints of cannonball attacks by armies of Jaipur can still be seen on the second gate. The fort has seven gates, including the famous Jayapol (victory gate) built by Maharaja Man Singh to commemorate his victories.

Tamil Nadu

Meenakshi Temple

Madurai, Tamil Nadu

Description

The Meenakshi Amman Temple is a historic Hindu temple dedicated to goddess Meenakshi, a form of Parvati. The temple forms the heart and lifeline of the ancient city of Madurai, with its towering gopurams (gateway towers) visible from most parts of the city.

History

Though the temple has been mentioned in Tamil literature for centuries, the current structure was mostly built during the reign of Thirumalai Nayak (1623–55). The temple complex spans 14 acres and features 14 magnificent towers, with the southern tower rising to over 170 feet.

Shore Temple

Mahabalipuram, Tamil Nadu

Description

The Shore Temple is a complex of temples and shrines overlooking the Bay of Bengal. It's one of the oldest structural (versus rock-cut) stone temples of South India and represents the final phase of Pallava art.

History

Built during the reign of Narasimhavarman II (700–728 CE), the Shore Temple is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Originally, there were seven temples along the shore, earning it the Dutch sailor name of "Seven Pagodas," but only one remains intact today, revealing ancient maritime connections.

Book a Visit

Brihadeeswarar Temple

Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu

Description

The Brihadeeswarar Temple, also called Rajarajesvaram or Peruvudaiyar Kovil, is a Hindu temple dedicated to Shiva. It is one of the largest South Indian temples and an exemplary example of fully realized Dravidian architecture.

History

Built by Raja Raja Chola I between 1003 and 1010 AD, the temple is part of the UNESCO World Heritage Site known as the "Great Living Chola Temples." The temple's vimana (tower above the sanctum) is 216 feet high, making it one of the tallest in South India.

Kerala

Bekal Fort

Kasaragod, Kerala

Description

Bekal Fort is the largest fort in Kerala, situated on the Malabar coast. This keyhole-shaped fort spread over 40 acres offers a spectacular view of the Arabian Sea from its tall observation towers, which were used to spot enemy ships.

History

Built in the 17th century by Shivappa Nayaka of Bednore, the fort later fell into the hands of Hyder Ali, then the British. The fort was primarily built for defense, as evidenced by the many underground tunnels leading to the sea and the storage facilities for arms and ammunition.

Paradesi Synagogue

Kochi, Kerala

Description

The Paradesi Synagogue is the oldest active synagogue in the Commonwealth of Nations. Located in Jew Town in the Mattancherry section of Kochi, the synagogue features hand-painted Chinese tiles, Belgian chandeliers, and a collection of antique copper plates with inscriptions.

History

Built in 1568 by Spanish-speaking Sephardic Jews, the synagogue was partially destroyed during Portuguese raids in the 17th century, then rebuilt by the Dutch. It houses the copper plates of privileges given to Joseph Rabban, the earliest known Cochin Jewish leader, by the Chera ruler Bhaskara Ravi Varman.

Mattancherry Palace

Kochi, Kerala

Description

Also known as the Dutch Palace, Mattancherry Palace is a Portuguese palace featuring Kerala murals depicting Hindu temple art, portraits and exhibits of the Rajas of Kochi. The palace is famous for its extraordinary collection of mythological murals, considered some of the best in India.

History

Built by the Portuguese in 1555 and presented to the Raja of Kochi, the palace was later renovated by the Dutch in 1663, hence the name "Dutch Palace." The murals depict the Ramayana, Mahabharata, and episodes from Puranic literature, painted using natural pigments and vegetable dyes.

Odisha

Konark Sun Temple

Puri, Odisha

Description

The Konark Sun Temple is a 13th-century CE sun temple dedicated to the Hindu sun god Surya. The entire temple was designed in the form of a chariot with 24 wheels, pulled by seven horses. The temple showcases the exquisite Kalinga architecture.

History

Built by King Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga Dynasty, the temple is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. According to local legend, the temple possesses a powerful magnet at its top which causes ships to crash. Konark means "corner" and "sun" in reference to its location and dedication.

Lingaraja Temple

Bhubaneswar, Odisha

Description

The Lingaraja Temple is an ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva and is one of the oldest temples in Bhubaneswar. The temple represents the Kalinga architecture and showcases the blend of the Nagara and Dravidian architectural styles.

History

Built in the 11th century by the Somavanshi dynasty, the temple rises to a height of 180 feet. The presiding deity is Harihara, a combined form of Vishnu and Shiva. Non-Hindus are not allowed inside the temple, but can view it from a viewing platform nearby.

Udayagiri and Khandagiri Caves

Bhubaneswar, Odisha

Description

The Udayagiri and Khandagiri Caves are partly natural and partly artificial caves of archaeological, historical and religious importance. Located on two adjacent hills, these caves feature elaborate carvings and sculptures from the 1st century BCE.

History

The caves were carved out as residential blocks for Jain monks during the reign of King Kharavela. The most important cave is the Rani Gumpha (Queen's Cave) in Udayagiri, which is a double-storied monastery with beautiful carvings of dancers and musicians.

Gujarat

Rani ki Vav

Patan, Gujarat

Description

Rani ki Vav (The Queen's Stepwell) is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the most magnificent stepwells in India. The stepwell was built as a memorial to King Bhimdev I by his widowed queen Udayamati in the 11th century.

History

Constructed in the complex Maru-Gurjara architectural style, it features more than 500 principal sculptures and over a thousand minor ones combining religious, mythological and secular imagery. The stepwell was flooded by the nearby Saraswati River and silted over until excavated by the Archaeological Survey of India in the 1980s.

Champaner-Pavagadh

Panchmahal, Gujarat

Description

The Champaner-Pavagadh Archaeological Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that contains ruins from many different eras, from prehistoric times to the 15th century. The site includes fortifications, palaces, religious buildings, residential areas, and water installations.

History

The site's history spans from the 8th to the 14th centuries, but the majority of ruins date from the 15th century when it was the capital of Gujarat. The Jami Masjid (Great Mosque) built in 1513 is one of the most important historical mosques in Gujarat, representing a perfect blend of Hindu and Islamic architecture.

Sun Temple Modhera

Modhera, Gujarat

Description

The Sun Temple at Modhera is a Hindu temple dedicated to the solar deity Surya. Built on the bank of the river Pushpavati, it stands as a masterpiece of ancient Indian architecture with its grand architecture and intricate stone carvings.

History

Built in 1026-27 CE during the reign of Bhima I of the Chaulukya dynasty, the temple is designed so that the first rays of the sun fall on the image of Surya during equinoxes. The temple complex consists of a Gudhamandapa (shrine hall), Sabhamandapa (assembly hall) and Kunda (reservoir), all perfectly aligned along an east-west axis.
